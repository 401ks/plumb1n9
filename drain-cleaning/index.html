Why Does My Drain Keep Getting Blocked?

One of the most common questions homeowners ask is: "Why does my sink or shower keep clogging even after I've cleared it?"

In many cases, the blockage you see is only part of the problem.

Kitchen drains often become restricted because grease, cooking oil, food scraps, and soap residue slowly stick to the inside of pipes. Over time, the pipe diameter becomes smaller and water flow slows down.

Bathroom drains are usually affected by hair, soap scum, toothpaste residue, and personal care products. Even when the visible blockage is removed, buildup deeper in the pipe can continue to cause problems.

For outdoor drains and sewer lines, tree roots are a surprisingly common cause of recurring blockages. Roots naturally seek moisture and can enter tiny cracks in underground pipes. Once inside, they continue growing and eventually restrict wastewater flow.

Signs You Need Professional Drain Cleaning

  • Water takes longer than normal to drain
  • Gurgling sounds from sinks, showers, or toilets
  • Bad smells coming from drains
  • Frequent clogs in the same location
  • Water backing up into other fixtures
  • Multiple drains becoming slow at the same time

When multiple fixtures are affected, the problem is often deeper within the drainage system rather than inside a single sink or shower drain.

Are Chemical Drain Cleaners Safe?

Many homeowners try chemical drain cleaners before calling a plumber. While these products may temporarily improve drainage, they often fail to remove the actual blockage. Repeated use can also damage older pipes and may make future repairs more difficult. For stubborn or recurring drain problems, professional cleaning methods are generally safer and more effective because they remove the buildup rather than simply creating a small opening through it.

How Often Should Drains Be Professionally Cleaned?

For most homes, a professional inspection and drain cleaning every 12–24 months can help prevent major blockages and unexpected plumbing emergencies. Properties with older plumbing systems, large families, restaurants, cafes, and commercial kitchens may require more frequent maintenance due to higher usage.
